Missing values are often missing as sets of variables. This implies that there is an explanation of missingness that tends to affect a number of variables simultaneously. This suggests that seeking to understand such an explanation may resolve many missing values.

#### Goal

The goal of this card is to visualise missingness into sets and thereby explore explanations common to the major sets.

#### Actions

Below are some actions that follow from a better understanding of the structure of the missing values.

-   Gain understanding of the causes of missing values.
-   Design manual imputation strategies for some variables.
-   Create shadow variables for membership of certain missingness sets.
-   Drop variables that are excessively missing. Drop variables responsibly. Check the variable's importance.
-   Drop observations that are excessively missing. Drop the observations responsible. This may result in too few usable observations.

#### Examples
> In the context of on-line purchasing, missing delivery address fields may be explained by parcel pick-ups.  
> In the context of mining, missing assay results (across a range of tests) might be due to their perceived low chance of being commercially viable.  
> In the context of criminology, missing crime data may be due to the staff-resource levels available at the time (and the low severity of the crime.)
